Understanding Air Quality

What is AQI?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized way to report air quality levels. It transforms complex air quality data into a simple number that helps people understand how clean or polluted the air is and what associated health effects might be a concern.

Health Impacts

Poor air quality can affect your health in many ways. Short-term exposure can cause irritation of the eyes, nose and throat, while long-term exposure may lead to respiratory diseases, heart disease, and even lung cancer. Children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing conditions are most vulnerable.

Protection Tips

  • Check air quality forecasts daily
  • Limit outdoor exercise when AQI is high
  • Use air purifiers indoors
  • Wear N95 masks in hazardous conditions
  • Keep windows closed during high pollution
